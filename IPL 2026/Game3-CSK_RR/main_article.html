Turning Points

Three moments defined this match. The first was the powerplay collapse: CSK ended 6 overs at 38 for 4, with Samson, Gaikwad, Mhatre, and Short all back in the pavilion. No top-order batsman survived the new ball.

The second turning point was over 8 — Jadeja's double strike. Sarfaraz (17 from 12, the brightest batter in the top seven) fell lbw in over 7, then Dube fell immediately after. 51 for 5 became 57 for 6 in four balls. CSK's brief middle-order resistance — Sarfaraz and Kartik Sharma adding 13 — was erased in a single over. From here, the match was about damage limitation.

The third turning point, and by far the most spectacular, came in the second innings. Sooryavanshi's arrival at the crease. By the end of over 6, RR had 75 for 1 and needed 53 more from 84 balls. It was over before Jaiswal's unbeaten 38 was even half-complete.

That Henry Over

Over 2 of RR's chase. Matt Henry to Vaibhav Sooryavanshi. CSK needed a maiden, or at minimum, a wicket. What they got was something else entirely.

The first ball: full, on off. Sooryavanshi drives through cover — four. 21 for 0. The second: slightly shorter, angling in. Sooryavanshi back-cuts it; two more. Third ball: Henry tries the bouncer. Sooryavanshi is already moving across, his eyes on the ball the moment it leaves the hand. He pulls it over square leg. Six. 33. Fourth ball: Henry goes full-length. Sooryavanshi hits it back over his head — straight six, 14 metres over mid-on. Six. 39. Fifth ball: Henry attempts the yorker. Sooryavanshi makes room and drives it into the off-side for four. 43. Sixth ball: Henry, out of ideas, bowls short and outside off. Sooryavanshi cuts again. Three more.

That over yielded 19 runs from 6 balls, including two sixes and a four. At 43 for 0 after 12 balls, RR needed 85 from 108 — a rate of 4.72. The chase was functionally over. Henry finished with 40 from 3 overs. More than 30% of all runs RR scored came off one bowler.

The question isn't whether CSK should have pulled Henry after over 1. The question is whether any captain could have predicted that a 14-year-old would make a New Zealand international look like net bowling. Nothing about Henry's recent form — serviceable, control-oriented — suggested this. Sooryavanshi simply played an over that defied prediction.
