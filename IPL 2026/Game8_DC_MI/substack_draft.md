## Matchup 1: Sameer Rizvi vs Jasprit Bumrah

The most decisive tactical battle of the match. Bumrah conceded just 3 runs from 8 balls to Rizvi — a strike rate of 37.5 against a batsman whose overall SR was 176.47. That 79% SR suppression is the highest effective ratio in the match. Bumrah's lines were relentlessly off-stump at yorker length or back-of-a-length: nothing short enough to cut, nothing full enough to drive with authority. Rizvi, who savaged every other bowler in the attack, simply couldn't get to him.

The tactical question is whether Axar Patel should have bowled more through Bumrah-esque plans. He didn't — and the match showed why: Rizvi attacked the non-Bumrah overs with a SR of 266 vs Thakur and 258 vs Bosch. The captain's lesson is to find your Bumrah and bowl him at Rizvi early and often.

At the start of over 11, DC needed 77 from 54 balls — a required rate of 8.56. Manageable, but requiring control. Shardul Thakur had the ball. Rizvi was on 33 off 26 balls.

## Game Changers

**Sameer Rizvi 🏆** — 90 off 51 balls (7 fours, 7 sixes) SR: 176.47

Rizvi, DC's impact sub, turned a nervy chase into a one-sided exercise in authority. Joining at 73/3 with DC needing 90 from 60 balls, he dismantled Shardul Thakur and Corbin Bosch to score 78 of his 90 off just 45 balls after his arrival. His partnership of 78 with Miller in 43 balls made the match safe before over 17.

**Mukesh Kumar 🏆** — 3-0-26-2, destroyed MI top order in overs 2-3

Mukesh Kumar's dismissals of Rickelton (9) and Tilak Varma (0) off consecutive balls in the third over effectively ended MI's innings as a contest. MI at 18/2 in 2.5 overs never recovered the platform that their batting depth needed.

**Suryakumar Yadav ** — 51 off 36 balls (3 fours, 2 sixes) SR: 141.66

SKY's 51 off 36 balls was the only innings that genuinely challenged DC's attack, and his partnership with Rohit — 53 runs in 41 balls — was MI's only sustained attacking phase. His dismissal to Ngidi's lbw in over 16 at 122/5 immediately made the target look below-par.

**Jasprit Bumrah ** — 4-0-21-0, 13 dots, economy 5.25

Despite going wicketless, Bumrah conceded just 21 off 4 overs (5.25 economy, 13 dots) and was DC's one phase of sustained pressure. The problem: DC scored so freely off the other bowlers — Thakur went for 41 and Bosch for 39 — that Bumrah's excellence was offset. A study in how one elite bowler cannot win a T20 alone.
