Matchup 1: Prabhsimran Singh vs Rashid Khan

Prabhsimran Singh arrived at the crease with four sixes already in the scorebook — three of them dispatched over the leg side with that low-grip, high-swing signature of his. Rashid Khan had watched that from the other end. He did not bowl the same ball twice.

Over 8 deliveries against Rashid, Prabhsimran scored 4 runs and was dismissed. Strike rate: 50.0 against a bowler he usually attacks at 154. The difference was control — Rashid's googly consistently landed in the zone that forced Prabhsimran either to defend or mistime, and at over 8.3, he chose wrong. The catch was clean. The over ended with Punjab at 83/2 instead of 83/1 and accelerating.

This dismissal matters because of what it denied. Prabhsimran at 154 SR would have taken PBKS to 110–120/1 by over 12. Instead, Connolly inherited a shakier scaffold. The collapse that followed was seeded here — not in Prasidh's ferocity, but in Rashid's quiet accumulation of pressure two overs earlier.

Everyone except Cooper Connolly.
