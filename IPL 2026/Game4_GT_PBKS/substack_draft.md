## Matchup 3: Cooper Connolly vs Prasidh Krishna

**[IMAGE 5: Matchup chart — upload Image_05_Matchup3.png]**

Prasidh Krishna was devastating. In the passage from over 12 to over 15, he dismissed Shreyas Iyer, Shashank Singh, and Marcus Stoinis — reducing PBKS from 110/2 to 118/6. His economy was 7.25 across four overs. He bowled to everyone who came in and took their wicket.

Everyone except Cooper Connolly.

Against Prasidh, Connolly faced 10 balls and scored 18 — SR 180.0 vs his match SR of 163.6. Prasidh's length was wrong for him. The fuller balls that curved back to dismiss right-handers were sitting up for Connolly's powerful arc through mid-on. Connolly drove three of them. He was seeing it like a football.

This matchup is the fulcrum of the entire match. Had Prasidh gotten Connolly — who was at 38 when Stoinis fell — PBKS would have been 118/7 with Jansen and Bartlett needing 45 from 33 balls. Instead, Connolly survived. And then he launched.

## That Prasidh Over

Over 14. Prasidh Krishna, running in from the New Chandigarh end. PBKS 110/3. Still 52 needed. Still winnable.

Ball 14.2: Shreyas Iyer. Half-volley, angled in. Iyer attempted the flick — mistimed it — straight to deep midwicket. 110/3 becomes 110/4 with no extra runs scored. Prasidh pumps his fist. The over hasn't ended.

Ball 14.4: Shashank Singh. Three balls later. The same angle, the same seam position. Shashank sees the swing and goes for the ramp over third man — but the ball doesn't move as late as he expects, and it goes straight to slip instead of over it. 113/5.

Two deliveries. Two wickets. GT's players converged in the outfield. Prasidh walked back to his mark with the kind of unhurried confidence that only comes from knowing exactly what you're doing.

Those two balls at 14.2 and 14.4 are why this match had a last-over finish instead of a PBKS canter. Prasidh got everyone. He just couldn't get Connolly.
