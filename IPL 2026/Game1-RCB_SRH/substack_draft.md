## That Duffy Over

Jacob Duffy's four-over spell — 4-0-22-3 — is the number that defines this match. Five-point-five runs per over. At the M. Chinnaswamy Stadium, in the opening match of IPL 2026, against a batting lineup that includes Travis Head and Ishan Kishan. That is not a T20 number. That is a Test match economy at a venue where the ball routinely disappears into a Karnataka night.

The method was specific. The scrambled-seam back-of-length delivery, angled into the body, rising sharply at the chest. Head saw it and pulled — into the hands of Phil Salt at deep square leg. Abhishek saw it and tried to cut — into Jitesh Sharma's gloves. Nitish Reddy saw it and pulled aerially — into midwicket's lap. Three different batsmen. Three different technical responses. Three identical outcomes. Duffy had decoded the SRH top order before they decoded him.

He was pulled from the attack at 6.6 overs when Devdutt Padikkal came on as RCB's Impact sub — a strategic decision that felt uncomfortable in retrospect. At the time, RCB were dominant and needed Padikkal's bat in the chase. Duffy walked off with 22 runs from 24 balls. He finished as Player of the Match, as he deserved. The only question is whether he'd have taken five had he stayed on.

## The Architecture

The load-bearing partnership in this match — the one that made everything else possible — was Ishan Kishan and Heinrich Klaasen's 97-run stand for the 4th wicket (53 balls, SR 183.0). SRH were 29/3 in over 4 when Klaasen came in. They were 126/4 in over 13 when Shepherd removed him. That partnership lasted nine overs and covered the dead period of Ishan's innings when he was consolidating after the chaos. It transformed a match that might have ended at 140-odd into a 201-chase.

RCB's load-bearing partnership — Kohli and Padikkal's 101 runs for the 2nd wicket (45 balls, SR 224.4) — was its opposite: not a rescue act but an acceleration. Salt gone for 8, the score 9/1. Padikkal came in as Impact sub with the express brief to take the powerplay by the throat. He did exactly that, making 61 off 26 before Harsh Dubey overpitched at long-off range. Kohli played the Shiv Chanderpaul role: an anchor who could explode. By the time Padikkal left, the match was decided.

**1. Jacob Duffy (RCB)** — 4-0-22-3, Economy 5.50, 13 dots

Three wickets in 24 balls including SRH's entire top order — Head, Abhishek, and Nitish — for 22 runs. His 5.50 economy in a Chinnaswamy game is borderline supernatural. Player of the Match. **2. Virat Kohli (RCB)** — 69* off 38 balls, SR 181.57, 5×4, 5×6
Kohli doesn't do quiet. He never has. 69 not out in 38 balls in his IPL 2026 opener — composed until he wasn't, murderous when he needed to be. The final over against Harshal (6,4,4,4) was the kind of statement only Kohli makes at Chinnaswamy. Cricket's greatest centre-forward in his home ground. Season-opening declaration.

**3. Ishan Kishan (SRH)** — 80 off 38, SR 210.52, 8×4, 5×6 — captain's innings
SRH were 29/3 in the 4th over. The number wasn't there to collapse to. Ishan Kishan made himself the reason SRH got to 201. A captain's innings in every sense: he batted through chaos, preserved wickets when it mattered, and then released when the bowling got tired. His 80 built the tension that made this match watchable.

**4. Devdutt Padikkal (RCB)** — 61 off 26, SR 234.61, 7×4, 4×6 — Impact sub
Padikkal came on as an Impact sub at 6.6 overs and immediately changed the mathematics. By 8.4 overs he'd hit 61 from 26 deliveries including a 50 off 21 balls.
