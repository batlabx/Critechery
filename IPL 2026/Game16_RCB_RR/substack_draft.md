## The Story

On April 10, one batter's audacious 26 balls changed the entire complexion of an IPL match. Vaibhav Sooryavanshi walked in to chase 202 with the intention of settling for nothing less than dominance. In 26 balls, he scored 78 runs (7 sixes, 6 fours, SR 300), essentially winning the game single-handedly before he was caught at fine leg.

The catch? Two wickets in the same over (Sooryavanshi + Hetmyer) meant that Dhruv Jurel's cool 81* off 52 balls became the unsung hero, providing the stability RR needed after the double-wicket shock.

For RCB, 201/8 looked respectable until Sooryavanshi decided it wasn't.

This match epitomizes modern T20 cricket: one batter's audacious stroke-play can swing an entire match in 26 balls.
